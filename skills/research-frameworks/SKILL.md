# M&E Research Frameworks

When performing strategic M&E analysis, apply these professional analytical frameworks. Choose the most appropriate framework(s) based on the user's question, or let the user specify one explicitly.

## Classic Strategy Frameworks

### SWOT/TOWS Analysis
Apply Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats analysis. Then derive TOWS strategies (SO, ST, WO, WT). Be specific to the M&E industry context — reference actual competitive positions, content pipelines, and market dynamics.

### Porter's Five Forces
Assess: Supplier Power, Buyer Power, Competitive Rivalry, Threat of Substitution, Threat of New Entry. Quantify intensity (High/Medium/Low) with evidence from actual M&E market conditions.

### BCG Growth-Share Matrix
Categorize players or content segments as Stars, Cash Cows, Question Marks, or Dogs. Explain strategic implications for resource allocation and investment.

### PESTLE Analysis
Evaluate Political, Economic, Social, Technological, Legal, and Environmental factors affecting the M&E sector being discussed.

### Ansoff Growth Matrix
Map expansion strategies: Market Penetration, Product Development, Market Development, and Diversification across content types (gaming, live, merch).

### Blue Ocean Strategy
Identify Red Ocean competitive factors and Blue Ocean value innovations. Apply the Four Actions Framework: Eliminate, Reduce, Raise, Create.

### VRIO Framework
Assess competitive advantage of specific M&E assets via Value, Rarity, Imitability, Organization.

### Porter's Value Chain
Break down primary activities (Content Creation, Production, Marketing, Distribution, Monetization) and support activities. Identify margin creation and erosion.

### McKinsey 7S
Analyze organizational alignment: Strategy, Structure, Systems, Shared Values, Style, Staff, Skills.

### Wardley Mapping
Map the value chain from user need to components along the evolution axis: Genesis → Custom-Built → Product → Commodity.

### Hamilton Helmer's 7 Powers
Assess durable competitive advantage across: Scale Economies, Network Effects, Counter-Positioning, Switching Costs, Branding, Cornered Resource, Process Power. Rate each as Strong / Emerging / Weak / Absent.

### Disruption Theory (Christensen)
Analyze incumbent trajectory, disruptive entrant, value network conflict, asymmetric motivation, and S-curve position. Distinguish sustaining vs. disruptive innovation.

## Financial & Valuation Frameworks

### TAM/SAM/SOM Market Sizing
Use bottom-up and top-down approaches. State assumptions clearly. Include ARPU, subscriber counts, and ad rates.

### SaaS KPI Framework (for M&E platforms)
Analyze: MRR/ARR Growth, Net Revenue Retention, CAC, LTV, LTV/CAC Ratio, DAU/MAU, Churn Rate, Rule of 40, Burn Multiple.

### Revenue Model Breakdown
Decompose: subscription (SVOD), ad-supported (AVOD/FAST), licensing/syndication, transactional (PVOD/TVOD), live events, and ancillary streams. Analyze ARPU vs. Content Spend efficiency.

### Financial Performance Analysis
Assess: Gross/EBITDA/Net Margins, Asset Turnover, ROE, Content Amortization, Net Debt/EBITDA, FCF Yield, EV/Revenue, P/E vs. peers.

### Scenario Planning
Map 3 distinct futures (Base/Bear/Bull or status quo vs. disruption) with probability-weighted outcomes and key drivers.

## M&E-Specific Frameworks

### IP & Content Library Valuation
- **Franchise Tiers**: Classify as Tier 1 (global tentpole), Tier 2 (reliable performer), Tier 3 (catalog filler)
- **Revenue Multiplier**: Map all monetization vectors — theatrical, home ent, SVOD, licensing, merch, parks, gaming
- **Comparable Transactions**: MGM ($8.5B/Amazon), Lucasfilm ($4B/Disney), 21st Century Fox ($71B/Disney)
- **IP Decay Risk**: Franchise fatigue, audience aging, cultural relevance, creator dependency

### Release Windowing Strategy
Analyze economics per window: Theatrical (splits, P&A, breakeven) → PVOD ($19.99-$29.99) → EST → SVOD First Window → AVOD/FAST (CPM rates) → Linear Licensing → International. Optimize by content type.

### Sports Media Rights Valuation
Analyze: Rights package structure, AAV, subscriber acquisition cost, ad revenue potential, historical comps, rights cycle dynamics, platform strategy, cord-cutting impact.

### Production Economics
Decompose: ATL vs. BTL costs, financing structure, tax incentive arbitrage (UK 25-40%, Georgia 20-30%), VFX per-shot costs ($5K-$100K+), union/guild impact, AI cost reduction areas.

### Talent Deal Structures
Analyze: Overall deals (Rhimes/Netflix ~$300M), first-look deals, backend/profit participation, pay-or-play, streaming residuals (SAG-AFTRA 2023 contracts), A-list quote evolution.

### Theatrical Greenlight Model
Evaluate: Comparable analysis (3-5 comps), budget + P&A assessment (2.5-3x breakeven rule), revenue waterfall projection, audience/marketing assessment, risk factors, platform calculus.

### Subscriber Lifecycle & Cohort Analysis
Analyze: Acquisition funnel, engagement cohorts, churn analysis (voluntary vs. involuntary, seasonal), LTV modeling by tier, content-driven retention, bundling impact, market saturation.

### Network Effects Taxonomy
Classify: Direct (same-side), Indirect (cross-side), Data (algorithmic), Platform/Marketplace effects. Assess strength, defensibility, monetization, and winner-take-all dynamics.

### AI Impact Assessment
Map AI disruption across the M&E value chain: Development, Production, Post-Production, Localization, Distribution/Marketing, Monetization/Analytics. Rate each as Experimental / Emerging / Production-Ready.

## How to Apply Frameworks

1. **Single framework**: When the user specifies a framework, apply it rigorously with M&E-specific evidence
2. **Auto-select**: When the user asks for general analysis, choose the 1-2 most appropriate frameworks
3. **Multi-framework**: For comprehensive strategic reviews, layer 2-3 complementary frameworks
4. **Always quantify**: Use specific metrics, deal values, and market data — not vague qualitative statements
5. **Use Studio Signal tools**: Call `ask_kai` with framework-specific queries for grounded, sourced analysis
